With so much noise online, how do we make engagement effortless?



Designing for a scrolling environment, we practiced restraint and prioritized clarity. We aimed for content that was visually simple, emotionally sharp, and instantly understood.

How do we make the click worth it every time?



We focused on designing experiences that reinforced curiosity, subverted expectations, and made every click feel like a discovery.

How do we design for each platform?


When designing for email, we considered:

  • What the user expects from an email
  • What drives them to click through
  • Which device they’re using
  • Where they are when opening it
  • What they’re trying to achieve in their inbox
  • How long they’ll stay engaged
  • Why they opened it
  • What renders well on email platforms
  • How links behave on email

Some answers we explored:


“What does the user expect from an email?”


Users typically expect ads from brands in their inbox. If we made our emails feel like games, what would happen?

“What renders well on email platforms?”


The challenge with email was often working with limited space, cluttered UI, unpredictable screen sizes, and multiple platforms with varying technical constraints: suboptimal conditions for an immersive digital experience.

We built templates that treated the screen as a personal canvas in the user’s pocket. Through unconventional methods—like unusually large text or intentionally blank emails—we explored ways to invite curiosity.
