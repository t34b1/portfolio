I completed this project to refine my end-to-end UX workflow and deepen my product design skill set.

Key focuses included:


  • Conducting user research, usability testing
  • Mapping information architecture and user flows
  • Applying component-driven design to support scalable, modular systems
  • Building a reusable component library

Preface

I started this project with lots of ideas and built out two user journeys in parallel. Over time, I realized focusing on one would’ve made the experience clearer, saved time, and led to stronger design choices.

I began with very simple screens, but testing showed people expect a baseline of features— things they don’t always verbalize, but still notice when missing. That taught me about visual and functional hierarchy in screen-based products, and how it differs from graphic design.

If I did this again, I’d aim for fewer screens with sharper focus, depth and stronger execution.

With rising costs and stagnant wages, more people are splitting expenses, yet most budgeting tools are built for individuals

Our app helps groups track shared expenses, budgets, and savings goals in real time, perfect for those with joint finances or big plans.

User research: Key findings

Participants find it challenging to navigate different spending habits, approaches to money, & tools.

They struggle to incorporate budget management into their daily schedules.

In groups, they are flexible on personal budget if there is prior agreement.
Methodology
I interviewed five working adults aged 18 to 40 to explore how they manage shared expenses, their emotions around it, and what excites them about group savings.

Problem statement


Ashley needs a budgeting app anyone can learn and use together.

Why?


She frequently plans group weekend trips and splits the cost among friends.
She worries about group conflicts from different financial preferences.

Problem statement


Natalie needs to stay on budget with her fiancé, even when they’re apart.

Why?


She plans to save for a house and start a family with her fiancé.
She worries her fiancé won’t stick to their budget when they’re apart.

Problem statement


Elliot needs a way to manage ongoing expenses with his ex-partner.

Why?


He's a busy paramedic who juggles co-parenting with his personal life.
His unpredictable schedule makes it difficult to coordinate shared responsibilities.
